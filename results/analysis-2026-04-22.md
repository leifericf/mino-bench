## Where the remaining cost sits

The mino eval "floor" is now ~1.5-2us per form (down from ~6us). The cost at
this floor is distributed roughly evenly between:

1. eval_impl dispatch work (bounds checks, type switch, special-form cache
   probes)
2. eval_value on the head (env_get walk)
3. eval_args cons allocation + arg eval
4. apply_callable (env_child, bind_params, body eval)

Further reduction at this floor would require architectural changes that
don't fit mino's minimalist C99 ethos: inline caching of call sites, ahead-
of-time compilation of forms to bytecode, or a JIT. None are justified by
the current profile.

## What was measured but not shipped

- alias_resolve hash table: qualified symbols are rare in hot paths; the
  linear scan of the ~10-entry alias table is not a measurable contributor.
- map_get_val keyword fast path: already solved by the pointer-eq check at
  the top of mino_eq (val.c:417), which catches interned keyword lookups.
- Keyword hash caching: the one hash per map get is small relative to the
  eval floor and costs a struct-layout change to collect.
